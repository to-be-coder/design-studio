# Derive before invent

**The move.** When an identity or system already exists — a brand, a product UI, an inherited
codebase — codify it *faithfully* before proposing anything new. Redesigning it was nobody's
brief.

**When it applies**
- Client-brand work: brand guidelines, existing sites/apps, Figma libraries → derive the tokens,
  don't invent a palette.
- Extending an existing product: its current patterns are the baseline pages, not obstacles.
- Any "add a feature" brief where the temptation is to quietly restyle the host.

**Cost**
- Derivation from messy sources (old Figma files, inconsistent CSS) produces a *draft*, not truth —
  review it with the owner before it becomes law.
- Faithful codification can expose real inconsistencies in the source. Surface them as findings;
  don't silently "fix" them into the derived system.

**The tell that you're violating it:** the deliverable looks more like your taste than their
identity, and no decision entry says the client asked for that.
